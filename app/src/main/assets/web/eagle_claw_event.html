Introduction
Introduction
The rich legacy of SRISTI gets a new expression in manual robotics. Here is an event that stimulates minds to innovate themselves and practically realize their ideas to achieve new heights. As you all know about the Eagle, it has sharp visions and can achieve great heights, but, more importantly it has a strong pair of Claws which help them to snatch almost anything they want anytime. We have this manual competition which is all about the perfection and efficiency. Follow the path as fast as possible, clinch the objects and place them perfectly while maintaining a sheer control over the zipline and survive the atmosphere.
Problem Statement
The task is to devise and fabricate a manually controlled (wired/wireless) robot that can traverse an arena, by using a zipline, picking up packages and placing them perfectly in the slot.
Team Specifications
1. A team may consist of a maximum of 4 members.
2. Students from different educational institutes can form a team.
3. All students with a valid identity card(college id/library id) of their respective educational institutes are eligible to participate.
Bot Specifications
1. Each team is allowed to have only one bot (wired/wireless).
2. Bot must be within 35x35x45(in cm).
3. Flying or any other method of mobility which leads the robot to lose contact with the rope(i.e the zipline) is not allowed.
4. The bot must be fully manual with all powering and motoring mechanisms self contained.
Arena Specifications
1. The arena for Eagle Claw consists the dimensions 300x100x100(in cms), refer to the diagram for better understandings.
2. There will be three boxes initially in the first round of the event. Each boxes of different colours and posses the dimensions 8x8x8(in cm). Hook mechanism can be used to lift the boxes as hooking threads will be connected with the boxes.
3. There will be a platform of 20cm width 50 cm from the ground, where the boxes will be present.
4. There will be holes of different colours and of dimensions 10cm length, 10cm width and 4cm height, where the blocks have to be placed.
5. Zip line (or the rope) will be placed above the platform (Robots will be using the zip line only and will not be able to get supported by the platform where the boxes remain).
Rules
Pre Game Setup
Each team will be allowed a slot of 5 minutes to adjust their machines on the test track. Just before the run, teams will get a chance to adjust their bot according to conditions of the arena.
Game Procedure
1. There will be two elimination rounds viz. Round Robin and Finals.
2. A maximum of 3 restarts will be given to each team.
3. Immobility (if robot is unable to travel for 15 seconds) would lead to restart followed by disqualification (after 3 restarts).
4. If the robot topples from the rope the bot will be placed from the start and it would affect the points collected earlier.
Round Robin
1. One run will be given to each team (along with 3 restarts).
2. For placing each box of same colour in same colour hole 50 points will be awarded. If the box of different colour is placed on a different colour hole 20 points will be deducted.
3. If a robot topples during the run, each time 20 points will be deducted.
4. For each restart 20 points will be deducted.
5. For completing the run successfully with only 1 restart, an additional 75 points will be awarded.
6. For completing the run without any restart will cause an additional point of 100.
7. For successful completion of the task with 3 restarts will see the team earning an extra 50 points.
Finals
To make life difficult for the eagle (i.e, the bot) aquatic effect will be included in the finals. The finals will see the boxes (with same dimensions as Round Robin) floating on a pool of water. The pool will be of the same dimension as the total setup in the round robin.
1. One run will be given to each team (with 2 restarts).
2. For placing each box perfectly 50 points will be awarded.
3. If a bot topples during the run, unfortunately the bot will be eliminated.
4. For each restart 30 points will be deducted.
5. For completing the task without any restart will cause an additional 100 points.
General Rules
1. Organisers will not be responsible for any alteration in the arena by the previous machines on the arena.
2. Any team that is not ready at the time specified will be declared DISQUALIFIED on spot.
3. The machines could be checked for their consistency before the run and would be discarded if found inconsistent with specifications.
4. Any robot that causes damage to the arena shall be DISQUALIFIED on spot.
5. Organisers’ decision will be treated as final and binding on all.
Prize
Certificate Policy
1. Certificate of Excellence will be provided to the participants who qualify for the FINALS.
2. Certificate of Participation will be provided to all the participants taking part in the event.
3. The teams which get disqualified due to disobeying any of the rules provided will not be entertained with any kind of certification.
